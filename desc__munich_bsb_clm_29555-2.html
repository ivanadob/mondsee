Munich, Bayerische Staatsbibliothek, Clm 29555/2 (olim: Clm 29085a)
Library catalogueShow TEI-XML
Ansegisus: Collectio capitularium

Parchment — Two fragments from a rotulus fols. — 350 × 240 mm — Upper Austria or Mondsee (?) — s. IX1

Fragment - Bischoff supposed it is produced in Upper Austria and found similarities with the script of the Landperht-Homiliar Bischoff 1980, 36. The fragment is a contemporary excerpt from all canons of the five councils from the year 813 (Mordek 1986).

Support: From the rotulus are preserved two fragments. The length of the original leaf is unclear (current length: 36 lines, ca. 350 mm). The width of the written text is almost entirely preserved.

Layout: Written space: 240 mm 10 mm

Script: Carolingian minuscle. Rubrics in Capitalis rustica. At the beginning of the chapter prolonged capital letters.

History: Served as binding waste (pastedowns) in Clm 6371 (Freising s. X), detached in 1876.

Selected literature: Hauke 2013, 17. — Bischoff 1980, 11, 36. — Bischoff 2014, 293, Nr. 3484. — Mordek 1986, 33-36 (facsimile). — Neumüller-Holter 1959, 26.

1r Capitularium collectio (lib. II, cap. XXXI-XXXIX: Canones from the councils of Mainz in 813 and ) Ansegisus. (Hauke 2013 suggests that this was a draft version using two exemplars hence the different chapter numbers.)

1v (Blank.)
